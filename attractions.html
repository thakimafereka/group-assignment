Top Attractions

Thaba-Bosiu

A historical mountain fortress important to Basotho heritage.

It was the political and spiritual heart of the Sotho Kingdom,because the plateau was never conquered, it provided a safe haven for displaced people during the Difaqane wars, allowing Moshoeshoe I to unite them into the Basotho nation. Today, it remains a sacred national monument and the burial site of the royal family and a place with rich history..

Semonkong Adventure

Maletsunyane waterfall, one of the highest and most powerful single-drop waterfalls in Africa.A spectacular,roughly 192 meters into a massive basalt gorge. It is celebrated as one of the highest single-drop waterfalls in Southern Africa. Adventure lovers can also experience the famous abseiling activity near the waterfall, known as one of the world’s highest commercial abseils. Tourists enjoy photography, camping, birdwatching, and learning about Basotho culture from local communities. The area is a perfect destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and visitors wanting to experience the beauty of Lesotho. Hiking trails around the falls provide amazing views of mountains, rivers, and valleys.

Afri-Ski Mountain Resort

Southern Africa’s only ski resort. In winter you can ski and snowboard; in summer it’s for hiking, mountain biking, and scenic chairlift rides. Located high in the Maluti Mountains of Lesotho, it transforms into a winter sports hub during the colder months (June to August), attracting visitors from across the region for skiing, snowboarding, and snow play. Outside of winter, the resort shifts into an adventure destination with mountain biking, hiking, quad biking, and scenic high-altitude relaxation. Its combination of dramatic mountain scenery, seasonal snow, and year-round outdoor activities makes it a standout destination for both adventure seekers and tourists looking for something different in Southern Africa.
