Travel Stories

Hiking Maloti Mountains

You start in Underberg, KZN, South Africa. The pass itself is a 9km steep gravel 4x4 track with 45 hairpin bends. No regular car will make it. Most tourists book a 4x4 tour. On the way up you’ll see waterfalls, valleys, and Basotho herders. At the top, the air is thin and cold even in summer, and the pub serves hot meals and craft beer with a view over both countries.

Semonkong Adventure

What to expect on the way: Semonkong is about a 3-4 hour drive from Maseru on a mix of tar and gravel roads. The last 20km is rough, so a 4x4 is best, especially in wet season. You’ll pass tiny mountain villages, herders with sheep and goats, and views that open up over entire valleys. Once there, you can abseil down the falls, hike to the base, or just sit on the rim with tea from the local lodge.

Thaba-Bosiu Cultural Village

Only 30-40 minutes from Maseru on a good tar road. The drive is easy and flat at first, then you climb up to the plateau. You’ll hike 30 minutes up to the plateau top with a guide. Expect stories of the 19th-century wars, views over the lowlands, and a chance to see traditional Basotho huts and crafts at the cultural village below.
